Hanson is a distinguished professor in physics at the University of California at Riverside. She did her PhD at MIT in 1973 and her research is interested in Experimential High Energy Physics. She spent sixteen years at SLAC, first as a research assistant and then as a permanent staff member. Whilst there, Hanson participated in the discovery of the J/psi meson and tau lepton. Her work led to the first evidence for quark jet production in electron-positron annihilation,for which she was awarded the 1996 Panofsky Prize with Roy Schwitters. Hanson has ranked the eighth top female scientist in 2022. Hanson joined UCR in 2002. She has conducted experiments at the Large Hadron Collider ‚Äî the world‚Äôs most powerful particle accelerator ‚Äî in Geneva, Switzerland. Specifically, her research has involved the Compact Muon Solenoid, or CMS, a general-purpose detector. The CMS experiment is one of the largest international scientific collaborations in history.

Hanson is a fellow of the American Physical Society and the American Association for the Advancement of Science. She is the recipient of a Guggenheim Fellowship as well as the W.K.H. Panofsky Prize from the American Physical Society. She is the author or coauthor of numerous publications in peer-reviewed journals on several topics in particle physics, such as the Higgs boson, the Standard Model, neutrinos and muons, and the CMS experiment. She received her doctoral degree in experimental high energy physics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
